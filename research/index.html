Peer-Reviewed Publications

2025

  1. Who deserves student finance? Results from a survey experiment in six advanced capitalist economies
    with Eloisa Harris, Alyssa M. Taylor, Flavia Fossati, Juliana Chueri, Mia Katharina Gandenberger and Carlo M. Knotz
    Journal of Ethnic and Migration Studies, 2025
    Summary

    Using survey experiments in six countries, we find that immigrant students are judged as less deserving of financial aid than natives. High-achieving immigrants can overcome this penalty, but working harder does not help. The immigrant penalty is driven by right-wing voters, while left-leaning respondents treat immigrants equally.

2024

  1. Cash-Based Interventions Improve Multidimensional Integration Outcomes of Venezuelan Immigrants
    with Achim Ahrens, Marine Casalis and Dominik Hangartner
    World Development, 2024
    Summary

    Evaluating a cash transfer ( $190) to vulnerable Venezuelan immigrants in Peru, we find that the intervention improves overall integration by 0.12–0.28 standard deviations, driven by gains in economic, social, and navigational dimensions. The cash transfer also boosts self-employment by 2–5 percentage points, with self-employed beneficiaries achieving higher income gains than wage workers. Effects diminish sharply with household size, highlighting the need for household-adjusted payments.
